### Starlink: Dominant but Under Pressure

Total constellation capacity sits at roughly **450 terabits per second**, with Gen2 satellites delivering 80‚Äì100 Gbps each. And yet: for the first time, SpaceX is cutting prices and running promotions. The reason is sitting on a launchpad.

### How Many Constellations Can the Market Support?

The old consensus was 2‚Äì3. The new reality: probably **4‚Äì6**, because LEO constellations have become quasi-strategic national assets. Geopolitical importance distorts normal market economics ‚Äî governments are willing to fund or subsidise their own or allied constellations for sovereignty and security.

Meanwhile, demand is nowhere near saturated. Analysis using the Non-GEO Constellations Analysis Tool shows mega-constellations "struggle to reach even 10% of the addressable market" ‚Äî there's room for multiple players to grow simultaneously.

### Southeast Asia Angle

Starlink is already live in Thailand, Philippines, Indonesia, Vietnam, and Malaysia. Amazon Leo has cited SEA as high-priority. For Indonesia's **17,000+ islands** and the Philippines' archipelago, LEO is genuinely transformational ‚Äî no fiber build-out could economically reach every island. AST SpaceMobile's direct-to-cell model could extend 4G/5G-equivalent coverage to unserved rural areas at a fraction of the cost of dedicated terminals.
