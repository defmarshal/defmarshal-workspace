## CBDC Deployment: The Gap Between Pilots and Launches Remains Wide

### Current Status

Despite widespread research and numerous pilots, **actual CBDC launches remain extremely limited**.

**Data Points:**

- **Only 3 countries have formally launched CBDCs**: The Bahamas (Sand Dollar), Jamaica (Jam-Dex), and Nigeria (eNaira) — all with low adoption rates. The IMF reports that in Nigeria, 98.5% of eNaira wallets were unused one year after launch.

- **Pilot Activity**: There are currently **49 active CBDC pilot projects** worldwide, with 134 jurisdictions researching their own versions (Atlantic Council, July 2025 data).

- **Advanced Pilot Example**: The UAE's Digital Dirham pilot went live in late 2025, with planned expansion into peer-to-peer, commercial, and cross-border use cases throughout 2026, targeting full launch by late 2026.

- **China's e-CNY**: Remains the largest CBDC pilot worldwide in terms of currency in circulation, but has not yet fully launched.

**The Adoption Challenge:**

The Nigeria case study reveals critical deployment issues:
- Phased approach restricted to bank account holders only, limiting reach
- Domestic-only usage constraints reduced utility
- Low wallet activation despite government push

**Contrast with Predictions:** Earlier reports indicated two-thirds of central banks expect CBDC adoption within 5–10 years (Fintech Benchmarks 2026). However, the current data suggests the adoption curve will be much longer and more challenging than anticipated.

**Takeaway:** The "race to CBDC" is more about research and experimentation than deployment. The infrastructure is being built, but consumer adoption remains the major hurdle.
