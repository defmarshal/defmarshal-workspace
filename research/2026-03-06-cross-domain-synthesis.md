## Banking & Fintech: CBDCs vs. Reality

### The Adoption Gap

Despite aggressive research and numerous pilots, **CBDC deployments remain extremely limited**:

- Only **3 countries have formally launched**: Bahamas (Sand Dollar), Jamaica (Jam‑Dex), Nigeria (eNaira). All exhibit low adoption rates — Nigeria reported 98.5% of eNaira wallets unused one year after launch.
- **49 active pilots** worldwide, with 134 jurisdictions researching (Atlantic Council, July 2025).
- **UAE Digital Dirham** pilot (late 2025) targets full launch by late 2026, expanding to peer‑to‑peer, commercial, and cross‑border use.

### Why the Gap?

The Nigeria case highlights common pitfalls:
- Phased rollout restricted to bank account holders → limited reach
- Domestic‑only usage constraints → reduced utility
- Inadequate wallet activation despite government push

In contrast, **open banking infrastructure** is advancing faster. More jurisdictions are mandating standardized API access to consumer financial data, effectively making open banking the default. This is enabling a new generation of embedded finance and AI‑driven financial services.

**Looking ahead:** The "race to CBDC" is more about building rails than driving adoption. The next 5 years will likely see incremental expansion rather than disruptive transformation.
