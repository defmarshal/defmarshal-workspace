## Findings

Based on recent research and industry trends (primarily 2023-2024), here is a concise summary of developments related to the concept of a **Real-Time AI Service Economy: A Framework for Agentic Computing Across the Continuum**.

### Core Concept
The framework envisions a decentralized, autonomous ecosystem where specialized AI agents (software entities with goals, tools, and the ability to act) discover, negotiate, and compose services in real-time across a distributed computing continuum—from cloud data centers to edge servers and on-device (IoT/endpoint) processors.

### Key Recent Developments & Trends

1.  **Shift from Monolithic to Multi-Agent Systems (MAS):**
    *   **Development:** The industry is rapidly moving beyond single, large LLMs toward collaborative **multi-agent systems**. Frameworks like **LangGraph**, **AutoGen**, and **CrewAI** enable developers to build teams of specialized agents that can plan, critique, and execute complex tasks by dynamically assigning roles.
    *   **Significance:** This is the foundational "agentic computing" layer, proving the technical viability of autonomous agent collaboration.

2.  **Orchestration Across the Computing Continuum:**
    *   **Development:** New platforms and protocols are emerging to manage agent deployment and workflow across heterogeneous environments. This includes:
        *   **Edge/Cloud Coordination:** Tools like **Kubernetes (K8s) for Edge** (K3s, KubeEdge) and cloud provider services (AWS Greengrass, Azure IoT Edge) are being adapted to orchestrate AI inference workloads and agent lifecycles across the cloud-edge-device spectrum.
        *   **Latency-Driven Routing:** Systems are being designed to automatically route agent tasks to the optimal node (e.g., a real-time vision agent on a local edge device vs. a complex planning agent in the cloud) based on latency, cost, and data privacy requirements.
