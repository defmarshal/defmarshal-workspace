## 1. Terminology Matters — Semi-Solid vs All-Solid

- **Semi-solid-state (hybrid)**: Reduces liquid electrolyte but does not eliminate it entirely (e.g., ~5% less liquid than conventional). Easier to manufacture with existing infrastructure. The MG4 Anxin Edition uses this approach.
- **All-solid-state**: Replaces liquid entirely with solid electrolyte (sulfide, oxide, halide, or polymer‑ceramic composite). Enables lithium‑metal anodes for higher energy density but faces greater manufacturing and interface challenges.

China’s draft national standard is formalizing these definitions, reducing marketing confusion.

## 6. Technical Reliability — What Must Still Be Solved

Laboratory breakthroughs have addressed many headline issues, but manufacturing reality remains the bottleneck.

### Interface Stability

- Lithium plating and dendrite formation at lithium‑metal/electrolyte interface.
- 2025 Nature Energy study: engineered solid interfaces allow higher plating currents without dendrites.

### Mechanical Brittleness

- Solid electrolytes can crack under volume changes.
- Nature Communications (2025): defect‑based toughening for halide electrolytes.

### Interphase Chemistry

- Long‑term degradation via unstable interphases.
- Joule studies (2025–2026): electric‑field‑modulated interphases improve durability in lithium‑metal/anode‑free cells.

### Manufacturing Yields

- Sulfide electrolytes require ultra‑high purity and moisture control.
- Pilot lines (Honda, Nissan) now focus on reproducibility and cost, not just performance.
- Factorial’s dry‑cathode architecture aims to simplify formation and reduce environmental impact.
