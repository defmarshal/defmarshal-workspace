## 2. Launch Cadence and Market Share

- **SpaceX**: 80+ launches in 2025, 90+ projected for 2026; >80% of global mass to orbit.

- **Arianespace**: ~8–10 launches/year (Ariane 6 transition).

## 3. Reusability Impact

- **Falcon 9 first stage reuse** (average 15+ flights) reduces cost per launch dramatically vs. expendable.
- **Fairing recovery** (netting on ships) adds further savings (~$6M per fairing).
- **Vulcan** plans to recover first stage engines ( SMART reuse) but not entire booster; expected reuse after 2027.
- **Ariane 6** remains expendable; ESA evaluating future reusable concepts (Themis).
- **Rocket Lab** recovering Electron first stages (helicopter catch) aims for reuse by 2026–2027; expected cost reduction 30%.
- **Starship** fully reusable; target launch cost <$10M; if achieved, <$100/kg to LEO – game changer.

## 4. Smallsat Rideshare Boom

- **Constellation deployments**: Starlink Gen2 (4,500 sats), Kuiper (3,236), OneWeb (2,000) use dedicated rideshare slots on Falcon 9 (SpaceX) and others.
- **Dedicated rideshare missions**: SpaceX Transporter (twice monthly), ESA’s “Rideshare” on Ariane 6, Rocket Lab’s scheduled rideshare.
- **Benefits**: smaller sats get affordable access; launch providers fill excess capacity; customers benefit from frequent options.
- **Market size**: Smallsat launches projected to reach 1,000+ satellites in 2026; rideshare accounts for ~70% of smallsat deployments.

## 6. Challenges & Risks

- **Supply chain constraints**: engines (Raptor, BE-4) production bottlenecks.
- **Regulatory**: FCC and international licensing delays for large constellations.
- **Market volatility**: Overcapacity could drive prices down further, hurting smaller providers.
- **Reusability reliability**: recovery, refurbishment, and certification cycles affect flight rate.
- **Environmental concerns**: carbon footprint of many launches; industry exploring green propellants and reusability benefits.
